The Silence of the Lawns

Sat 07 July 2018
By Greg McClure

A City of Irvine post from September 25, 2017, touts Irvine’s retention of “Safest City” status:

The information released today shows that Irvine has the lowest rate of violent crime of any City in the nation with a population of 250,000 or more. This is the 12th consecutive year Irvine has earned the Safest City designation.

The FBI’s Annual Uniform Crime Report measures Part 1 crime, which includes murder, rape, robbery, aggravated assault, burglary, larceny-theft, auto theft and arson.

As someone who’s been living here for eleven years — a confession that dismays me as I write it — the post’s capitalization of “City” in “any City in the nation” is nearly as telling as the FBI’s roseate designation. It is to being a “City” that Irvine originally aspired and has ultimately succeeded at becoming, the capital C signifying the town’s reach for utopian distinction.
