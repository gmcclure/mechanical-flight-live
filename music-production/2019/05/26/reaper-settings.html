Reaper Settings for macOS

26 May 2019 — music-production — reaper

[DRAFT]

I like Reaper. It’s funky — the most finished unfinished piece of software I’ve encountered. A basic installation, however, benefits from tailoring.

Arrangement

Media Explorer

The media explorer works well for me when it’s on the right side. Grab the panel’s tab and drag it there. On the default theme, I like the media explorer to occupy the entire right-hand side, even when the docked mixer is visible (toggle ⌘-M).

Routing Matrix

Where goes the media explorer, so goes the routing matrix’s nation. Drag that over to the right-hand side, too.

Transport

Right-click the transport and change Docked transport position to Top of main window.

Theme

Theming is probably a rabbit hole. I think (completely unscientifically) a theme’s greatest impact comes from the first 5 to 10 seconds of viewing, and then a user starts becoming accustomed to whatever’s in front of them — if whatever’s in front of them is useful. Reaper is often called out for not being pretty, but other DAWs are changing their skin, subtly or dramatically, with every major version. Nothing’s ever finished. Theme your hours away.

For now, I’m often in the default theme. Used with track colors I prefer, it works fine. I’m also frequently using these, in order of preference:

  • Janne2016_v104_digitalfixRemix
  • Janne2014 EZ Mod 2 Flat
  • Janne2016II_v10

But the default theme comes with a nice selection of layouts.
